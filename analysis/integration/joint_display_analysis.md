# Joint Display Analysis of AI and Supervisor Feedback

## Quantitative Analysis

- Feedback length
    - AI feedback is significantly longer.
- Comparison of Rubric Scores
    - Accurate
        - AI feedback tended to be higher but not significant in CLMM.
    - Clear direction
        - AI feedback achieved a higher score.
    - Criteria based
        - AI feedback tended to be higher but not significant in CLMM.
    - Prioritization
        - AI feedback tended to be higher but not significant in CLMM.
    - Supportive
        - AI feedback tended to be higher but not significant.
- Consistency of Feedback Quality
    - AI feedback quality is more consistent, whereas supervisor feedback quality is more variable
- Correlation between feedback length and score
    - While there was no correlation between length and quality in AI feedback, longer submissions received higher scores in supervisor feedback.
- Identification of Feedback Source
    - All evaluators were able to determine whether the provider of all feedback was an AI or a supervisor.

## Qualitative Analysis

- Theme: "Adherence to the feedback criteria and structure"
    - AI feedback closely follows the rubric with a clear PNP structure and prioritized, actionable points, sometimes feeling formulaic, while supervisors often deviate from the rubric, offering context-rich but less prioritized or incomplete action items.
- Theme: "Continuity and consistency"
    - AI maintains tight continuity with the written record, yielding concrete text-level suggestions but missing broader clinical context, whereas supervisors align with lived clinical experiences yet frequently fail to engage the portfolio’s specific content.
- Theme: "Perspective as a clinician"
    - AI provides strong structure but thinner clinical reasoning and occasional inaccuracies, while supervisors contribute expert judgment, pathophysiology, and consequential priorities, albeit with potential bias or authority gradients
- Theme: "Quality of the Japanese language"
    - AI language often appears machine-translated, inconsistent, or formulaic, whereas supervisors’ wording is typically natural, human, and relationship-affirming.
- Theme: "Text Length"
    - AI feedback is long and example-rich, aiding clarity but risking verbosity and diluted priorities, while supervisors’ comments are often too brief, sometimes omitting essentials, yet can be highly effective when concise and targeted.
